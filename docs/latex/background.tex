\subsection{Human Inspiration}
Neural Networks are a leading heuristic for general function approximation given how well they are able to lend themselves to arbitrarily complex non linear correlations between high dimensional spaces.
Their structure is heavily inspired by similar structures in cerebral connections.
Human cognition and artificial intelligence thus are known to face common challenges, such as managing knowledge across diverse tasks without losing prior knowledge gain.
Humans and other cognitively advanced organisms generally use \textbf{integration} to share knowledge between tasks through social structures.
They share tribal knowledge by teaching new cohort members by showing and doing.
Modern AI systems use the opposite approach, \textbf{compartmentalization}, by focusing the training and testing of such systems into specific "contexts" to avoid interference of clashing data sets.
Even omni-model AIs use a small ecosystem of these focused AIs that the orchestrator merely summarizes back to the user, giving the illusion of a single model. \footnote{FIND REF}
This is largely done to prevent a phenomenon known as "catastrophic forgetting" which is most likely to happen when an AI is presented with a continuous stream of new information.

Compartmentalization can still occur in humans, however.
For example, an extreme example thereof can be disassociate identity disorder (DID), where memories are split between identities.
