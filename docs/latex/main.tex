\begin{abstract}
    Hexagonal Neural Networks (HexNNs) are a novel neural network topology that exploits the rotational symmetry of the hexagonal lattice to enable directional training and inference and induce shared representational influence between orientations.
    This yields a new operator, rotation, that can be integrated into the training loop and this paper establishes a baseline benchmark set that tests the performance of the new operator.
    Early results show that basic rotation sequences in the training loop provides stable performance that is better than or comparable to MLP baselines on synthetic datasets.
    Further, complex benchmarks show that with a proper synchronization and rotation scheduling strategy, this setup can provide six shared weight networks that can be trained and evaluated in parallel, each with a different abstract representational space that influence each other to escape local optima and find better solutions than their equivalent MLP counterparts.

\end{abstract}
